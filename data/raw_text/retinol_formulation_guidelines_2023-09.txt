Introduction

appearance. This product is recommended for use in anti-aging and anti-
wrinkle products, products for even skin tone care, age spot treatment, skin 
re-sculpting and smoothing products, and slimming and body sculpturing 
products.

FORMULATING 
 
Solubility 
 
 
RETINOL is hardly soluble in water but miscible with oils and fats. 
 
Incorporation 
 
 
RETINOL is sensitive to Oxygen. It is therefore recommended to manufacture retinol 
formulations under an inert atmosphere or to reduce the presence of Oxygen during 
handling and production to the possible minimum. 
 
RETINOL is sensitive to higher temperatures (> 40°C). Therefore, when producing an 
emulsion, it is recommended to add RETINOL below 40°C after the emulsification 
step is complete. 
 
To improve the stability of RETINOL in the formulation it is recommended to add 
chelating agents, UV absorbers like PARSOL® GUARD and Antioxidants like Mixed 
Tocopherols 95, or STAY-C® 50. 
 
Indeed, STAY-C® 50 has recently shown high performances to recover Retinol 
content at room temperature and 40°C. Be aware, that it is a salt, that may impact 
the stability of electrolytes sensitive systems. 
 
RETINOL tolerates high shearing. 
 
Incompatibilities 
 
 
In general, RETINOL shows a very good compatibility with all groups of raw materials 
like emulsifier systems, emollients and waxes, preservatives, hydrocolloids, 
sunscreens, pigments, detergents and other commonly used substances. 
 
Recommended pH of the final formulation: 4.5 – 8.0, it can be adjusted with 
commonly used neutralizing agents. 
 
Nevertheless, the compatibility always depends on the complete final formulation. 
The formulator has to monitor potential incompatibilities during the time of storage 
with each formulation.

Storage of formulations 
 
It is recommended to fill finished formulations under inert atmosphere in non-
transparent packaging. 
 
It is recommended to keep formulations without daylight and to store below 25°C.
